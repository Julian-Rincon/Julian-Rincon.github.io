About me

I don't build demos. I build systems that run.

I'm a 9th-semester (final term) Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence student at Universidad Sergio Arboleda, operating at the intersection of machine learning engineering, distributed data systems, and autonomous AI. My work ships to real infrastructure: AWS EMR clusters, Lambda functions, production RDS instances, and a Linux workstation where the abstractions stop.

On the data side, I architected ShopStream, an end-to-end AWS Big Data pipeline processing 2.5M synthetic events through S3, Lambda validation, EMR/PySpark analytics, Glue ETL and a Flask API. On the decision-systems side, SAVI v2 combines MDP, Q-Learning and Deep Q-Networks into a consensus policy that autonomously approves or escalates real estate valuations, with an XGBoost baseline scoring R²=0.9609. I also maintain NEXUS, a personal AI system that runs my own machine day to day: multi-provider LLM routing, dozens of self-discovered skills, and a home/GCP deployment. It's closed-source by design, since it touches my finances and home systems, so the public repo documents the architecture instead of shipping the code.

Beyond software, I care about showing up on bigger stages than the ones I started on. At 17 I represented my school in Ecuador at an international competition in Barcelona, and years later that same instinct, to compete and build somewhere bigger than my own classroom, is what's pointing me toward an internship now and an MSc in Germany after. More on that (and on USABOT) in Leadership & Impact below.

SAVI v2 — Autonomous Real Estate Valuation

End-to-end ML system built with a 3-person team (Valeria Larea, Nicolás Garzón, Juan Niño) for our Machine Learning course: a full RL pipeline combining MDP Value Iteration, Q-Learning (8,000 episodes), and a Deep Q-Network (PyTorch) trained on 20,203 properties.
