About me

I don't build demos. I build systems that run.

I'm an 8th-semester Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence student at Universidad Sergio Arboleda, operating at the intersection of machine learning engineering, distributed data systems, and autonomous AI. My work ships to real infrastructure — AWS EMR clusters, Lambda functions, production RDS instances, and a Linux workstation where the abstractions stop.

On the data side, I architected ShopStream, an end-to-end AWS Big Data pipeline processing 2.5M synthetic events through S3, Lambda validation, EMR/PySpark analytics, Glue ETL and a Flask API. On the AI side, I built NEXUS, an autonomous personal AI system that manages the machine, not just conversation — 5-tier LLM routing (Cerebras, NVIDIA NIM), 38 self-discovered skills, a hybrid GCP/local deployment, an MCP server exposing capabilities to other AI agents, and safety-gated automation for home, finance, and dev workflows. And on the decision-systems side, SAVI v2 combines MDP, Q-Learning and Deep Q-Networks into a consensus policy that autonomously approves or escalates real estate valuations.

Beyond software, I integrate hardware and product thinking: I led USABOT's 1st-place win — 3D CAD prototyping, sensor integration, and the commercial pitch that sold the idea to judges. And I led the technical development of WarmiTics, the 1st-place winning project of the Wisibilízalas international competition (Universidad Pompeu Fabra) as Technical PM and Lead Frontend/UX — representing my school in the Senior category at age 17 against teams from across Latin America and Europe. My long-term goal: an ML/Data Engineering internship now, an MSc in Germany next.
